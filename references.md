# Design References

Sites Vicky likes — use these to inform design decisions on tone, layout, and visual style.

---

## Gabriel Valdivia
https://www.gabrielvaldivia.com/

**What to take from it:**
- Generous whitespace, content-first layout — let the work breathe
- Card-based project display with clear visual hierarchy
- Clean sans-serif type system with strong size contrast between levels
- Neutral base palette, not prescriptive — accent colour pulled from content
- Light/dark mode handled elegantly as a system, not an afterthought
- Tone: direct and confident but conversational — "taming the chaos", not corporate

---

## With Conviction
https://withconviction.co/

**What to take from it:**
- Single-column vertical scroll, generous section spacing
- Colour used intentionally and boldly — not afraid of vibrancy
- Personality through small details: illustrated arrows, emoji, warm copy
- Tone: approachable and human, positions collaboration as the default mode
- Navigation kept minimal and uncluttered
- Says a lot with few words — copy punches above its weight

---

## Zef's Website
https://zefs.website/

**What to take from it:**
- Personality through copy — witty parenthetical asides ("Airbnb for cars", "That liberal newspaper") make the content memorable without visual gimmicks
- Work grouped by employer in a linked list, with date ranges — implicit navigation over explicit menus
- Restraint as a confidence signal — no imagery, no chrome, just content; the lack of spectacle is itself a statement
- Tone: self-aware and irreverent but credible — humour and professionalism coexist without one undermining the other

## Thibaud Allie
https://www.thibaudallie.com/

**What to take from it:**
- Editorial, magazine-issue framing elevates a portfolio beyond a list of projects — curation over completeness
- One project at a time via pagination — depth of engagement over breadth of display
- Candid about what didn't ship ("Unused and Archived") — honesty about curation builds more trust than showing everything
- Tone: understated confidence — location, title, spare copy; no over-explaining

---

## Belen Tenorio
https://www.belentenorio.com/

**What to take from it:**
- Grid of cards with subtle motion (animated previews) — dynamism as a light touch, not a performance
- Consistent card structure creates rhythm; the repetition is the design
- Monochromatic base palette — project visuals carry the colour, the container stays neutral
- Terse project labels (company, discipline, format) — the work speaks; description gets out of the way
